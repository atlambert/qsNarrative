The Quantified Self

Community & Context

Quantified Self (n).

externalizing our bodies through data to learn more about ourselves.

This process creates a bridge between our biological and personal information and the technical products in our lives.

Quantified Self is also a broader tech trend that has grown as sensors have become cheaper & more ubiquitous.

The quantified self is more than just tools, though; it's about asking good questions and finding smart answers.

It's easier than ever to find interesting patterns in commonly collected data; here, a person notices their weight increasing.

Based on our observations, we can start to ask new questions about ourselves to find correlations and trends; here, that an increase in visits to the local sandwich shop is correlated with weight gain.

Spreadsheet entries with qualitative data can be analyzed for patterns to explain why weight gain may have occurred.

All of these tools and questions often create some misconceptions;

Those three are part of a broader community.

In September 2008, Kevin Kelly, the founding editor of WIRED magazine, held the first quantified self meetup group. 28 people attended. After the Meetup, Kelly remarked, "I was blown away by the energy and fire-power of this group of pioneers. I learned tons, and more importantly, wanted to hear from each of you. Will have to do that again."

And ever since, the community has grown across the world.

The quantified self represents a diverse number of viewpoints on its aims.

Some in the community want it to be more rigorous and academic; others believe in a more casual approach.

In the end, it's probably beneficial that people are thinking critically and asking questions about data, regardless of background.

Quantified Self tools can be a security risk.

Today, it is unclear whether it is valuable to steal activity tracker data. There may be some value in the location data, however.

The data from your general purpose wearables (e.g., smartwatch) will be more valuable to steal than from your fitness tracker.

In the future, “grey market” data brokers may want to collect all the data possible about you to build more complete profiles for advertising.

The quantified self movement has a lot of opportunity for growth.

Many predict that quantified self will grow beyond the individual...

...to the quantified community.
